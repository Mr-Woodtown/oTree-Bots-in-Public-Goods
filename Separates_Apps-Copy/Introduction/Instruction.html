Public Goods Game Instructions

Overview

We will assign you a participant ID. It will not be linked to you personally. It can only identify demographic information that you provide over the course of the study. Please keep it with you at all times. You may write on it if you wish, but make sure you do not write over the ID number.

At the end of the experiment, we'll ask you to complete a brief survey. Your answers will remain confidential and will be used only to help us analyze the results—they won't be shared publicly and will remain confidential.

You will be playing a decision-making game that lasts 10 rounds. In each round, every participant will receive $1.00. You must decide how much of that $1.00 to keep for yourself and how much to contribute to a group pot.

Communicating to other participants is not allowed while the game is running. You forfeit all payout except your show-up bonus by doing so. Asking a question to a researcher at any time is allowed, though they may not answer your question in order to not reveal the mechanism of the game.

Round Flow

●     You receive $1.00.

●     You decide how much to contribute (anywhere from $0.00 to $1.00).

●      The total contributions are multiplied by 1.6.

●     The multiplied pot is divided equally among all players.

●      Your round payoff = (money you kept) + (your share of the pot).

This process repeats for 10 rounds.

You may leave the experiment at any time and receive your show-up bonus; however, you will forfeit any payout you would receive from the results of the game.

Treatment

There may be automated “bots” in your group. If there were no bots in your group, it would consist of 4 human participants. If there were 1 bot in your group, the remaining 3 participants would be human. If there were 2 bots in your group, the remaining 2 participants would be human. Should there be more than one bot, each would replace one human participant in the group. These bots, if present, value their own payout and follow a consistent strategy.

Before the start of the first round, you will be informed if your group consists of 4 human participants or 2 bots and 2 humans.

Important Notes

●     Each round is independent. You always start with a new $1 and no more.

●     Your decisions are private, but the group results (pot size and payouts) will be shown after each round.

●     There are no “right” or “wrong” choices.
